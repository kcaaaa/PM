# Jensen Huang - Life & Career Timeline

## Early Life

- **1963 Feb 17** — Born in **Tainan, Taiwan**
- **~1968** — Family moved to **Thailand** for father's work
- **~4.5 years old** — Received a **facial scar from a knife accident** at a night market in Thailand. The scar remains visible and is part of his personal story
- **1973 (age 9)** — Sent to the **United States** by his parents for better education. Through a mix-up, he and his brother ended up at **Oneida Baptist Institute** in Kentucky — a reform school, not the academic institution his parents intended
  - Was **bullied** by older students (many were troubled teens)
  - Assigned to **clean bathrooms** and do manual labor
  - Developed resilience and work ethic that he credits as formative
- **~1975** — Parents **immigrated to the United States**, settling in Oregon. Jensen finally reunited with family and attended regular school

## Education & Early Career

- **~1984** — Graduated from **Oregon State University** with BS in Electrical Engineering
  - Met his future wife **Lori Mills** in the engineering program (she was his lab partner)
- **1984** — Joined **AMD** as a chip designer
  - Learned **Mandarin Chinese** during this period (not his childhood language — he grew up speaking Taiwanese Hokkien and English)
- **~1985** — Married **Lori Mills** (they remain married, 40+ years)
- **~1985** — Left AMD, joined **LSI Logic** as a chip designer
- **1992** — Completed **MS in Electrical Engineering** from **Stanford University** (while working)

## NVIDIA Era

### Founding & Early Years
- **1993 Jan 25** — **Founded NVIDIA** at a Denny's restaurant in San Jose, California, with co-founders Chris Malachowsky and Curtis Priem. Jensen was **30 years old**. Initial funding: ~$600
- **1993-1998** — Multiple near-death experiences for the company. Early products (NV1, NV2) struggled commercially. The company nearly went bankrupt multiple times
- **1999** — **NVIDIA IPO** on NASDAQ
- **1999** — Released **GeForce 256**, marketed as the world's first GPU (Graphics Processing Unit), coining the term and defining the category

### Building the Platform
- **2006** — **CUDA released** — enabling general-purpose computing on GPUs. The decision that would ultimately make NVIDIA the AI infrastructure monopoly
- **2012** — AlexNet wins ImageNet using NVIDIA GPUs, triggering the deep learning revolution. NVIDIA's early bet on AI researchers begins to pay off
- **2016** — Jensen personally **delivered the first DGX-1** system to OpenAI, recognizing early that this startup would be pivotal to AI's future

### Strategic Expansion
- **2018** — **Huang's Law** proposed at GTC — the observation that GPU performance improves at more than 2x every two years, outpacing Moore's Law
- **2019** — **Mellanox acquisition** completed for **$6.9 billion** — Jensen's self-described "greatest strategic decision," adding high-speed networking to NVIDIA's platform
- **2020** — Announced **ARM acquisition** from SoftBank for $40 billion
- **2022** — ARM acquisition **failed** due to regulatory opposition from multiple jurisdictions. NVIDIA pivoted to developing its own ARM-based Grace CPU

### AI Boom & Global Influence
- **2024 Mar** — Delivered **Stanford commencement speech** ("I hope suffering happens to you," "greatness comes from character formed by suffering")
- **2024 May-Jun** — **Computex in Taiwan** — night market photos and videos went viral globally, cementing Jensen's cultural icon status
- **2025 Mar** — **GTC 2025 keynote** — announced Physical AI and Agentic AI visions, Blackwell architecture details
- **2025 May** — Stratechery Computex interview on platform strategy and sovereign AI
- **2025 Oct** — NVIDIA becomes the **first $5 trillion company** in history
- **2025 Dec** — **Joe Rogan Experience #2422** — brought AI discussion to the mainstream podcast audience
- **2025 Dec** — Named **Financial Times Person of the Year 2025**

### Peak Influence
- **2026 Jan** — **CES keynote** — unveiled Vera Rubin architecture, next-generation GPU roadmap
- **2026 Jan** — Awarded **IEEE Medal of Honor** — the highest recognition in electrical engineering
- **2026 Jan** — Appointed to **PCAST** (Presidential Council of Advisors on Science and Technology)
- **2026 Mar** — **Lex Fridman Podcast #494** — deep technical discussion on co-design and CUDA ecosystem
- **2026 Mar** — **GTC 2026 keynote** — introduced Token Factory concept, announced Groq LPU integration, revealed Feynman architecture
- **2026** — Named **Fortune #1 Most Powerful Person in Business 2025** (announced early 2026)
