# Ilya Sutskever - External Views & Criticism

## Praise

### Geoffrey Hinton (PhD advisor)
- "Very good raw intuitions" about deep learning.
- Expressed pride that "one of my students fired Sam Altman."

### OpenAI Colleagues
- Called "the prophet" internally.
- Described as "the most brilliant mind at OpenAI."
- "Quiet formidable mind behind the curtain" - the intellectual engine of OpenAI's research direction.

## Rivalry

### Yann LeCun
- Public rival on multiple fronts.
- "I told you so" stance on scaling limits - LeCun argued earlier that pure scaling would hit walls.
- Ongoing disagreements on architecture, approach to AGI, and the role of self-supervised learning.

## Criticism

### "Slightly Conscious" Claim (2022)
- **LeCun**: "Nope." - flat dismissal.
- **Tom Dietterich**: Accused Sutskever of "trolling."
- **Toby Walsh**: Argued the claim "damages realistic AI discourse."

### Safety Strategy
- Critics argue Sutskever's superalignment approach relies on an assumption of AI sentience - a philosophical contradiction (you need the thing you're trying to align to already have the properties you're trying to instill).
- Characterized by some as "relatively shallow" compared to more rigorous formal verification approaches.

### Scaling Position Change
- Critics note that when Sutskever announced "pre-training will end," others (notably Gary Marcus) had been saying this for years. The shift was seen by some as belated rather than prophetic.

### SSI Skepticism
- $32B valuation with ~20 employees.
- Zero products, zero revenue.
- Complete opacity about technical approach.
- Raises questions about whether the valuation is based on Sutskever's reputation alone or on genuine technical breakthroughs.
