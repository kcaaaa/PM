## Core Recurring Arguments (appears in >= 3 distinct sources)

### 1. "Compression = Understanding"
Good compression leads to unsupervised learning. Compression is prediction. A model that compresses data well must understand its underlying structure.

### 2. "Next Token Prediction = World Model"
Predicting the next token is not a shallow statistical exercise - it requires and produces genuine understanding of the underlying reality that generated the text.

### 3. Scaling Hypothesis --> SHIFT to "Age of Research"
- Early position: "Just make it bigger" - scaling compute and parameters is the primary path to capability.
- NeurIPS 2024: "Pre-training as we know it will end."
- 2025: Three eras framework: 2012-2020 (research era) --> 2020-2025 (scaling era) --> 2026+ (new research era).

### 4. Superalignment
Safety and capability must be developed together, not as separate tracks. Alignment framed as a generalization problem - can we get AI to generalize human values?

### 5. Data as "Fossil Fuel"
"We have but one internet." The supply of high-quality training data is finite and will become the binding constraint on pre-training as currently practiced.
