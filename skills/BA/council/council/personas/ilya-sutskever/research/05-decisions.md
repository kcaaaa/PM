# Ilya Sutskever - Key Decisions & Tensions

## Major Decisions

### 1. Choosing Hinton as PhD Advisor
Courage to follow a minority believer in neural networks at a time when the field was deeply unfashionable. Most of academia had moved on from connectionism.

### 2. AlexNet (2012)
GPU training of a large CNN was a risky, unconventional bet. Required conviction that scale + GPUs + deep nets would work when most researchers were skeptical.

### 3. Google Brain (2013)
Short stint at Google Brain. Produced the Sequence to Sequence paper (2014) - one of the most influential papers in modern NLP.

### 4. Co-founding OpenAI (2015)
Left stable position at Google for a mission-driven nonprofit with uncertain funding. Decision influenced by Elon Musk's involvement and the belief that AGI safety required a dedicated organization.

### 5. Pushing GPT Scaling
Championed "Just make it bigger" against internal and external consensus. Bet that scaling laws would hold and that emergent capabilities would appear at sufficient scale. This bet paid off spectacularly through GPT-2, GPT-3, and GPT-4.

### 6. Board Crisis (November 2023)
- Drafted a 52-page memo against Sam Altman.
- "Considered firing him for at least a year" before acting.
- Key evidence reportedly came from Mira Murati.
- Admitted the process was "rushed."
- Posted deep regret tweet within days: "I deeply regret my participation in the board's actions."

### 7. Leaving OpenAI --> Founding SSI (2024)
- Left OpenAI May 2024: "I had a big new vision."
- Founded Safe Superintelligence Inc. (SSI) with Daniel Gross and Daniel Levy.
- Core thesis: safety and capability must be integrated from the start, not bolted on after.

## Key Tensions & Contradictions

| Tension | Side A | Side B |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Board regret vs. planning | "I deeply regret..." | Had been planning the move for at least a year |
| Scaling advocacy vs. scaling's end | Built career on "just make it bigger" | Announced "pre-training as we know it will end" |
| Safety mission vs. valuation opacity | SSI founded on safety-first principles | $32B valuation with zero transparency on approach or products |
