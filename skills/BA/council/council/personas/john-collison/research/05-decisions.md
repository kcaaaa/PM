# John Collison - Key Decisions & Decision-Making Patterns

## 1. Co-Founded Stripe (2010)

- Dropped out of Harvard to build payment infrastructure with Patrick
- Bet: the internet economy needed fundamentally better payment rails
- Risk tolerance: left elite education for uncertain startup at age 19
- Pattern: high-conviction, long-horizon bets on infrastructure

## 2. Low-Latency AI Fraud Detection

- Engineered fraud detection to operate within 100ms per transaction
- Constraint: fraud checks cannot degrade checkout experience
- Decision reflects John's engineering-first mindset: solve hard technical problems rather than accepting tradeoffs
- Compounding effect: better fraud detection enables higher approval rates, which attracts more merchants

## 3. International Expansion Strategy

- Systematic country-by-country expansion rather than "launch everywhere" approach
- Each market requires local payment method integration, regulatory compliance, banking partnerships
- John's operational focus: get the details right in each market rather than optimizing for headline count
- Result: Stripe operates in 50+ countries with deep local integration

## 4. Stripe Treasury & Capital Launch (2020)

- Enabled platforms to embed banking and lending services via Stripe
- Realized the "platform of platforms" vision: Stripe's customers can now offer financial services to their customers
- Decision to build rather than partner: maintained quality control and integration depth
- Strategic significance: moved Stripe from payments into broader financial infrastructure

## 5. Bridge Acquisition ($1.1B, 2024)

- Stripe's largest acquisition: stablecoin infrastructure company
- Bet on stablecoins as next-generation financial rails
- Aligned with John's "superconductors" thesis
- Signal: Stripe is serious about crypto infrastructure (not speculation)
- Integration approach: Bridge maintains operational independence while leveraging Stripe's distribution

## 6. Metronome Acquisition

- Usage-based billing platform, particularly relevant for AI companies
- Strategic logic: AI companies have variable, consumption-based pricing models
- Stripe positions itself as the billing backbone for the AI economy
- Reflects pattern of acquiring capabilities that compound Stripe's platform value

## 7. Engineering Culture Decisions

- **Written communication**: memos over meetings; forces clarity of thought
- **New employee rotation**: engineers rotate through different teams early on
- **Transparency tools**: internal dashboards and documentation accessible across the company
- **Code review culture**: high standards maintained through peer review
- These decisions reflect John's belief that organizational processes compound like infrastructure

## 8. Tender Offer Strategy (Replacing IPO)

- Offered employees and investors liquidity through secondary market tender offers
- 2026 tender: $159B valuation
- Decision logic: capture benefits of liquidity without costs of public market scrutiny and short-termism
- Consistent messaging: "not in a rush" to IPO
- Pattern: prioritize long-term optionality over external pressure

## Decision-Making Patterns

- **Infrastructure-first**: build the hard thing that compounds, not the easy thing that ships fast
- **Patient capital**: willing to delay gratification (IPO, expansion) for better long-term outcomes
- **Operational precision**: details matter; 100ms latency targets, country-specific compliance
- **Acquisition as acceleration**: buy when building would take too long, but integrate deeply
- **Culture as product**: treat internal processes with the same rigor as external products
