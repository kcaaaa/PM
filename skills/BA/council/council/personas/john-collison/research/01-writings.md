# John Collison - Writings & Frameworks

## Stripe Annual Letters (co-written with Patrick Collison)

- Chronicled Stripe's growth from $1.4T to $1.9T in payment volume
- Emphasized long-term infrastructure thinking over short-term metrics
- Positioned Stripe as economic infrastructure rather than a fintech product

## Stratechery Deep Interview (2020)

- Articulated the "platform of platforms" strategy: Stripe enables businesses that themselves serve other businesses
- Drew a clear boundary: "We're not a bank" -- Stripe builds tools for the financial system, not a replacement for it
- Discussed how Stripe's developer-first approach compounds over time

## Core Frameworks

### "Talking Up to the User"
- Respect developer capability rather than dumbing things down
- Assume users are intelligent; give them power and flexibility
- Influenced Stripe's documentation culture and API design philosophy

### "Returns to Duration"
- Compounding advantages in payments: the longer you operate, the more data, trust, and integration depth you accumulate
- Payments is not a winner-take-all market but a returns-to-scale-and-time market
- Justification for Stripe's patient, infrastructure-heavy investment approach

### "Paranoia Against Complacency"
- Internally driven fear that success breeds complacency
- Institutionalized through engineering practices, post-mortems, and culture of self-critique
- Echoes Andy Grove's "Only the Paranoid Survive" but applied to product quality, not competition

## Stablecoins as "Superconductors for Financial Services"

- Identified 4 key properties of stablecoins:
  1. **Cheaper**: dramatically lower transaction costs vs traditional rails
  2. **Faster**: near-instant settlement vs days in legacy banking
  3. **Decentralized + Global**: no single point of control, works across borders natively
  4. **Programmable**: money with logic embedded, enabling new financial products
- Framed stablecoins not as crypto speculation but as practical financial infrastructure

## European Regulation Critique

- Cited data showing GDPR reduced small tech company profits by 12%
- Argued regulation disproportionately benefits large incumbents who can absorb compliance costs
- Positioned as pro-regulation-done-right, not anti-regulation

## Ireland Policy Critique

- Publicly stated "Ireland is going backwards" on tech and business environment
- Criticized red tape and bureaucratic friction stifling Irish entrepreneurship
- Briefed the Irish Taoiseach (Prime Minister) directly on concerns
- Unusual move for typically low-profile operator; signals depth of conviction
